About WOO

The World Olympiad Organisation (WOO) is dedicated to fostering academic excellence and nurturing young talents across the globe. Our mission is to provide a platform for students to showcase their knowledge, critical thinking, and problem-solving skills.

We conduct seven distinct Olympiads covering Physics, Chemistry, Biology, Science, Mathematics, Junior Science, and Hackathon competitions. Each Olympiad is carefully designed to challenge students appropriately for their academic level.

Our competitions are divided into two categories: Epsilon for classes 5-7 and Zeta for classes 8-10, ensuring age-appropriate challenges and fair competition.

Our Olympiads

WOO conducts seven specialized Olympiads to challenge students in different scientific disciplines:

WPO Physics Olympiad

Challenge your understanding of fundamental physics concepts and problem-solving abilities.

WCO Chemistry Olympiad

Test your knowledge of chemical principles, reactions, and laboratory concepts.

WBO Biology Olympiad

Explore the fascinating world of living organisms, from cellular biology to ecosystems.

WSO Science Olympiad

A comprehensive test covering Physics, Chemistry, and Biology with slightly easier questions.

WMO Mathematics Olympiad

Challenge your mathematical reasoning, problem-solving, and proof-writing skills.

WJSO Junior Science Olympiad

An integrated approach combining science, mathematics, and experimental concepts.

WHO Hackathon Olympiad

Apply your coding and problem-solving skills to real-world challenges in a timed competition.

Competition Categories

All Olympiads are divided into two age-appropriate categories with tailored difficulty levels:

Epsilon (Classes 5-7)
Zeta (Classes 8-10)

Epsilon Category Details

For students in classes 5-7, the Epsilon category provides an engaging introduction to Olympiad competitions with age-appropriate challenges.

Olympiad Duration Question Pattern
WBO, WCO, WPO, WSO, WJSO 45 minutes 25 SCQ (+2/-0.5) + 5 MCQ (+4/-2)
WMO 1 hour 25 SCQ (+2/-0.5) + 5 MCQ (+4/-2) + 1 Proof (0≤x≤10)
WHO 2 hours 5 problems (no negative marking, each 10 marks max)

Zeta Category Details

For students in classes 8-10, the Zeta category offers more advanced challenges to test deeper understanding and problem-solving skills.

Olympiad Duration Question Pattern
WBO, WCO, WPO, WSO, WJSO 1 hour 35 SCQ (+2/-0.5) + 5 MCQ (+4/-2)
WMO 2 hours 35 SCQ (+2/-0.5) + 5 MCQ (+4/-2) + 3 Proof (0≤x≤10)
WHO 2.5 hours 7 problems (no negative marking, each 10 marks max)
