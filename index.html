The Seven Challenges

Seven specialized Olympiads designed to foster deep understanding and critical thinking in core academic disciplines.

WPO (World Physics Olympiad)

A deep dive into the fundamental laws governing the universe, from classical mechanics to quantum phenomena. Participants will tackle problems that demand conceptual clarity and mathematical application.

WCO (World Chemistry Olympiad)

Exploring the composition, structure, properties, and change of matter. This Olympiad challenges students with reactions, stoichiometry, and understanding molecular structure.

WBO (World Biology Olympiad)

The study of life itself. Questions span from cellular biology and genetics to ecology and evolution, testing knowledge of living organisms and vital processes.

WSO (World Science Olympiad)

A holistic challenge covering Physics, Chemistry, and Biology. Designed for a broad assessment of core scientific principles with a relatively lower level of question difficulty compared to the specialized Olympiads.

WJSO (World Junior Science Olympiad)

An integrated challenge encompassing Science, Mathematics, and practical experimental reasoning. This test emphasizes interdisciplinary problem-solving and application.

WMO (World Mathematics Olympiad)

The pinnacle of logical reasoning and quantitative aptitude. This Olympiad includes complex problem-solving and rigorous mathematical proof-writing.

WHO (World Hackathon Olympiad)

A practical, time-bound challenge that tests coding, logic, and creative problem-solving skills. Participants must develop innovative solutions to real-world problems.

Mission Parameters & Scoring

The structure and scoring schema varies by grade level. Choose your category:

Epsilon Category (Classes 5-7)

WPO, WCO, WBO, WSO, WJSO (45 Minutes Each)
  • **Question Count:** 30 Questions Total
  • **Format:** 25 Single Choice Questions (SCQ) + 5 Multiple Choice Questions (MCQ)
  • **SCQ Scoring:** +2 Marks for correct, -0.5 Marks for incorrect.
  • **MCQ Scoring:** +4 Marks for correct, -2 Marks for incorrect.
WMO (World Mathematics Olympiad) (1 Hour)
  • **Question Count:** 30 Questions + 1 Proof Section
  • **Format:** 25 SCQ (+2/-0.5) + 5 MCQ (+4/-2) + 1 Proof Question
  • **Proof Scoring:** 0 to 10 marks (Partial credit possible).
  • **Proof Alternatives:** 2 alternative methods for proving will be given.
WHO (World Hackathon Olympiad) (2 Hours)
  • **Problem Count:** 5 Problems will be given.
  • **Scoring:** 10 marks maximum per problem.
  • **Negative Marking:** NO negative marking.

Zeta Category (Classes 8-10)

WPO, WCO, WBO, WSO, WJSO (1 Hour Each)
  • **Question Count:** 40 Questions Total
  • **Format:** 35 Single Choice Questions (SCQ) + 5 Multiple Choice Questions (MCQ)
  • **SCQ Scoring:** +2 Marks for correct, -0.5 Marks for incorrect.
  • **MCQ Scoring:** +4 Marks for correct, -2 Marks for incorrect.
WMO (World Mathematics Olympiad) (2 Hours)
  • **Question Count:** 40 Questions + 3 Proof Sections
  • **Format:** 35 SCQ (+2/-0.5) + 5 MCQ (+4/-2) + 3 Proof Questions
  • **Proof Scoring:** 0 to 10 marks per proof (Partial credit possible).
  • **Proof Alternatives:** 5 alternative methods for proving will be given.
WHO (World Hackathon Olympiad) (2.5 Hours)
  • **Problem Count:** 7 Problems will be given.
  • **Scoring:** 10 marks maximum per problem.
  • **Negative Marking:** NO negative marking.
